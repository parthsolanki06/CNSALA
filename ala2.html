Understanding the Avalanche Effect

In cryptography, the Avalanche Effect refers to a property of block ciphers and cryptographic hash functions. It occurs when a small change in the input (such as flipping a single bit) results in a drastic and unpredictable change in the output.

This demo highlights that change by comparing two inputs character-by-character. Even if you change just one lowercase 'a' to a capital 'A', the resulting hexadecimal string will look completely different to any observer.

Integrity Verification: This is why SHA-256 and SHA-512 are used to verify that files haven't been tampered with. If even one byte of a file changes, the hash will fail to match.
