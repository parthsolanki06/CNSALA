How Digital Signatures Work

A digital signature is a mathematical technique used to validate the authenticity and integrity of a message, software or digital document. It's the digital equivalent of a handwritten signature or stamped seal, but far more secure.

Integrity: If the message is altered after it is signed, the verification process will fail, signaling that the data was tampered with.

Authenticity: Since the private key is held only by the sender, a valid signature proves the message came from them.
